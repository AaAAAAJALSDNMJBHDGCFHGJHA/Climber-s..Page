Rules and Etiquettes:

1. Use Proper Gear

  • Harness & Helmet:Always wear a climbing harness and a helmet. Ensure they are properly fitted and certified.
  • Climbing Shoes:Use appropriate climbing shoes for better grip and control.
  • Belay Device & Rope:When roped climbing, use an approved belay device and rope with the correct strength rating.
  • Quickdraws & Anchors (for lead climbing):Check that all equipment is properly placed and locked before ascending.

    • 2. Communication

  • Climbing Calls:Use clear and agreed-upon commands (e.g., "On belay?", "Climbing!", "Take!", "Slack!").
  • Belayer-Climber Communication:Ensure the belayer and climber communicate well and understand each other's signals.

    • 3. Follow Proper Belaying Techniques

  • Attention: The belayer should be alert at all times, keeping an eye on the climber and responding to their needs.
  • Brake Hand: Always keep your brake hand on the rope, especially when feeding or taking in slack.
  • Backup Systems: For lead climbing, make sure there are backup knots or appropriate belay devices to prevent accidents.

    • 4. Check Everything Twice

  • Partner Check: Before starting, check your partner's gear (harness, belay device, knots, helmet, and shoes).
  • Knots & Gear: Ensure that all knots (especially the figure-eight) and carabiners are properly tied and locked.

    • 5. Climb Within Your Limits

  • Know Your Skill Level: Attempt climbs that match your current skills, and don't push yourself beyond what you’re capable of, especially without proper support.
  • Rest & Hydration: Take breaks when needed and stay hydrated, especially during outdoor climbs.

    • 6. Respect the Environment

  • Leave No Trace: Pack out all trash, avoid disturbing wildlife, and stay on marked trails when accessing climbing areas.
  • Minimize Impact: Use established anchors and avoid creating new ones unnecessarily. Keep the natural rock environment intact.
  • Chalk Use: Use chalk sparingly to avoid leaving unsightly marks on the rock.

    • 7. Understand Weather Conditions

  • Check Forecasts: Always review weather conditions before heading out for outdoor climbs. Avoid climbing in poor weather (rain, lightning, extreme cold).
  • Heat & Cold Safety: Dress appropriately for conditions and be aware of heat exhaustion or hypothermia risks.

    • 8. Follow Ethical Climbing Guidelines

  • No Chipping or Drilling: Avoid altering rock surfaces (no chipping or drilling holds) unless it's a designated sport route.
  • Route Ethics: Adhere to the ethics of the climbing community. For example, clean climbing routes mean no permanent modifications (like bolts) to the rock.

    • 9. Know When to Retreat

  • Risk Awareness: If conditions become unsafe or you feel uncomfortable, it's okay to retreat from a route. Safety always comes first.

    • 10. Learn & Practice Self-Rescue Techniques

  • Knowledge of Rescue Procedures: Be familiar with self-rescue techniques in case of emergency. Know how to tie essential knots, escape a belay, and rappel safely.

    • Specific rules by Climbing Types:

  • Bouldering: Always use crash pads and have spotters when necessary.
  • Sport Climbing:Pre-inspect anchors and quickdraw placements. Use proper lead climbing techniques.
  • Trad Climbing:Ensure all placements are secure, and avoid overloading cams and nuts.
